Heat Exchanger - Area Determination

A counter flow shell and tube heat exchanger is used to heat water that flows at a rate of 0.8 kg/s from 30 degrees C to 80 degrees C with hot oil entering at 120 degree C and leaving at 85 degree C. the overall heat transfer coefficient is 125 W/m2K. Calculate the heat transfer area required. Write a python program for this. Include a plot that shows the variation in area as a function of temperature to show the effect of inlet and exit temperatures of oil on area. Observations from the graph: 1. Moving from the lower bottom left to the lower bottom right in the graph (increasing oil inlet temperature while keeping oil outlet temperature constant) results in a decrease in the required heat transfer area. 2. Moving from the lower bottom right to the upper top right in the graph (constant oil inlet temperature while increasing the outlet temperature) results in a decrease in the required heat transfer area. 3. Moving from the lower bottom left to the upper top right in the graph (increasing oil inlet and outlet temperatures) decreases the required heat transfer area.
